Silence, Only Silence

by

MTTKTZ

begin
Kevin gathered his courage and opened the basement door. Silence. Only silence.
Kevin held his breath and listened. Silence. Only silence. He held his breath until the song of his blood turned to surf in his ears and let his breath out with a whoosh.
Kevin turned on his flashlight and peered at the splintery stairs leading down into the dark. Nothing moved but dust motes, no sound but the creak of his foot on the first stair and then -
No, silence. Only silence. Kevin played his flashlight around the basement and pushed the black back in a tight circle.
Kevin gripped the rail and stepped onto the second of twelve wooden steps. There was no beast. There was no beast. There was only a breaker box and a switch that needed to be flipped.
Kevin's breath thickened in his throat and he stepped down again. He strained to hear a clue, a signal, but there was just silence. Only silence.
On the fourth step Kevin spoke to himself to break the silence and joked and the joke died in his throat. He tapered off and shook his head.
Kevin felt the draft coming from upstairs chill the sweat on his neck and stepped down to the fifth step. The breeze increased and whistled through a cracked window upstairs.
When Kevin stepped onto the sixth of twelve steps the breeze pushed the door very slowly and then faster. The door slammed shut at the top of the stairs with a bang and Kevin stumbled down onto the seventh step.
Kevin was closer to the bottom of the stairs than he was to the top. There was no beast. He had a flashlight. It would be silly to go back up stairs to open the door, to wedge a chair in front of it. There was no beast and if there was you'd be foolish to turn your back on it and face up the stairs. Better to go down.
Kevin wasn't scared at all and so he stepped onto the eighth step and besides there was only silence down here.
Kevin cleared his throat and stepped onto the ninth step. The dark was darker. The silence was louder.
Kevin knew there wasn't a beast and there never had been. No one had ever mentioned it. And if there was a beast that came in the dark it could be discussed and there wouldn't be only silence on the subject. So Kevin stepped down.
Kevin's flashlight dimmed and he shook it. It brightened back and he quickly took two more steps down. He played the flashlight all around to check everywhere.
Kevin startled when the silence ended and his grip on the flashlight loosened. It fell down. Then there was only darkness and silence. Only silence.
